\ques{4}

\noindent A home baker sells three types of bread: plain, olive and raisin. One plain loaf is made using 2 ounces  of sugar and is sold for 4 dollars. One olive loaf is made using 2 ounces of sugar and is sold for 5 dollars. Finally, a raisin loaf is made from 3 ounces of sugar and is sold for 8 dollars. Last week, the baker used a total of 34 ounces of sugar and earned a total of 75 dollars.

\vspace{5pt}

\noindent \textbf{a)} \ Define all the necessary variables and set up the system of equations to determine how many of each type of bread were sold last week. \textbf{Do not solve.}

\vspace{5pt}

\noindent \textbf{b)} \ Given that the augmented matrix for the system reduces as shown below, give the general solution for the system. \ \ $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -1/2 & 10 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 7 \\ \end{array} \right]$

\vspace{5pt}

\noindent{c)} \ Find all possible realistic solutions.

\begin{answer}
\theques. \ a) \ $\begin{array}{rcl} x_1 & = & \text{\# of plain loaves} \\ x_2 & = & \text{\# of olive loaves} \\ x_3 & = & \text{\# of raisin loaves} \\ \end{array} \ \ \left\{ \begin{array}{rcrcrcl} 2x_1 & + &  2x_2 & + & 3x_3 & = & 34 \\ 4x_1 & + & 5x_2 & + & 8x_3 & = & 75 \\ \end{array} \right. $

b) \ $(10+\nicefrac{1}{2} \, t, \ 7-2t, \ t)$ \ \ c) \ $t=0 \ \rightarrow (10,7,0) \ \ t=2 \ \rightarrow (11,3,2)$
\end{answer}
